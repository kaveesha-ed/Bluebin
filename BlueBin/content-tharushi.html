The Dubai Reef Project

A living lab for marine restoration and ecotourism

Dubai Reefs is a pioneering project aimed at marine restoration and ecotourism. It serves as a floating living lab, fostering sustainable practices and environmental conservation.

  • Dubai Reefs
  • Ecotourism
  • Marine Institute

Why Dubai Reefs?

Dubai Reefs encompasses a sustainable floating community dedicated to marine research, regeneration, and ecotourism. It includes residential, hospitality, retail, educational, and research facilities.

It addresses the urgent need for sustainable coastal development in the face of climate change. It aims to serve as a model for other cities, demonstrating the benefits of integrating nature with urban infrastructure.

Discover Ecotourism in Dubai Reefs

A key feature of Dubai Reefs is regenerative ocean farming, a climate-friendly approach to food production and environmental restoration. Through innovative farming practices, the project seeks to enhance marine ecosystems while ensuring food security for local communities.

Dubai Reefs offers unique experiences in marine ecotourism and wellness, including floating eco resorts and immersive underwater activities. By promoting human and ecological well-being, the project aims to create a sustainable destination for travelers and locals alike.

Marine Institute

The Marine Institute in Dubai aims to promote marine science and conservation by providing a collaborative environment for public and private sectors. It will provide facilities for oceanographers, climate scientists, ocean farmers, marine educators, and the next generation of marine biologists. The institute will lead a global ocean cleanup initiative to eliminate plastic debris. Technology will be used in the construction of Dubai Reefs, including 3D printing and bioprinting. The institute will study methods to mitigate climate change threats to coral reefs and accelerate their population. It will also provide educational programs in marine conservation and blue carbon initiatives. The institute's mission is to better connect ocean ecosystem health to human health.
